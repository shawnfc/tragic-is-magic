My Story

Elexus Hunter was born in the heart of San Francisco, California, where she lived with her mother and two siblings. As the oldest in an abusive household, she had to take on adult responsibilities at a young age. Subjected to the streets, moving from house to house, and undergoing unrelenting abuse from both family and friends, she didn't know who to trust. Thus, she merely allowed her inner turmoil to become a tormentor that began to affect most every aspect of her existence. Anger lay within her young spirit and heightened into her early teenage years, as she faced more dysfunction within the foster care system.

Battling her own emotions, she began sacrificing friends and even family relationships in order to find peace. What could have easily been a tragedy became a magical tincture of sorts to propel her to a more promising place, beyond her horrific reality. She found a respite, or her purpose, where both her story could help and heal others. As such, her tragic became magic. Throughout this memoir, readers should view Elexus’ world through a lens of hope rather than despair. She shares her deepest secrets and agony to allow youth in similar conditions to understand that people who hurt people are doing so because they are themselves wounded, not because victims deserve it. Victims of any type of abuse should then understand that their journey was no accident. They should accept what God has allowed them to overcome as somewhat of a catapult rather than a crutch. Ultimately, they are not tarnished or tragic; their testimonies are, in fact, meaningful and magical.
